Early years

He was born in Stratford-Upon-Avon, Warwickshire, England and a son of prominent town official. As the 3rd child of 8 children, he received a good local education with heavy focus on grammar and classics. He married at age 18 to Anne Hathaway (she was 26), who was then pregnant with his child, and his 1st child was daughter, Susanna-born in 1583, them Twins, Hamnet and Judith born in 1585 but Hamnet dies at age 11.

Lost years

The period between 1585 (aged 21) and 1592 (aged 28)is known as the “Lost Years” because there are no documentary records of Shakespeare’s activities. Shakespeare’s personal, political and religious views are also a mystery.

Later years

He moved to London around 1591 and became an actor, working with the Lord Chamberlain’s company of players, later known as the King’s Men. His career bridged the reigns of Elizabeth I (1558–1603) and James I (1603–1625), a favorite of both monarchs.
